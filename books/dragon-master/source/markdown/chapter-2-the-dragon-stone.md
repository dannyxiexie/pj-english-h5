He follows Griffith into a room piles high with strange things. He sees bottles fill with colorful waters and powders. Griffith picks up a wooden box. The box is carved with pictures of dragons. "This is why you are here," he says, opening the box.

Drake peers inside. A green stone as big as his head glitters in the light. "This Dragon Stone tells me you have the heart of a dragon," says the wizard. He taps Drake's chest. "It d-does?" Drake asks. His eyes get big. "Yes. And those with the heart of a dragon become Dragon Masters for King Roland," Griffith says. Drake has heard stories about the magical Dragon Stone. But he has never believed they are real. Now all his questions come spilling out.

"How does the stone know I have the heart of a dragon? And how does you find me? And what's a Dragon Master? And why does King Roland want Dragon Masters?" "The stone is old and mysterious," Griffith says. "Even I do not fully understand its magic. A Dragon Master is able to connect with dragons. As for the king ... he is very fond of dragons. But he cannot control them." "So Vulcan isn't the only dragon?" Drake asks. The wizard grins. "No, he is not." He hands Drake a green stone on a gold chain. "This is a piece of the Dragon Stone," he says. "It will help you connect with your dragon."

## Page 21

Drake's heart jumps. My dragon? I get a dragon? he thinks. He slips the stone into his pocket. "Be careful, Drake," the wizard warns. "Dragons are dangerous. And even the Dragon Stone can't protect you from their powers." "What powers?" Drake asks. The wizard leads him away without another word.
