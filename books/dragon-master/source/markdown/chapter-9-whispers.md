# Chapter 9 - Whispers

## Page 56

WHISPERS And that is how to shine a dragon's scales," Griffith is saying later that week. "Remember, brush one scale at a time. No shortcuts." Rori sighs. "When can we go out again?" Drake is glad Rori has asked. They have been stuck in the Training Room for three days.

## Page 57

Drake likes learning about dragons, but he is used to being outside all day every day on his farm back home. He is starting to forget what the sun looks like. The wizard pats a tall pile of books on his desk. "There is much for you to learn first. We will go out again soon, Rori." Then a soldier comes in. He hands something to Griffith. The wizard smiles. "Drake, it is a letter for you." "Reads it out loud!" Ana says. Drake quickly nods to Griffith.

## Page 59

"Your mother sounds nice," Bo says. Drake's eyes start to burn. He holds back his tears. "Thanks," he says. "May I send another letter to tell her about the dragons?" "You must not say anything about the dragons, " Griffith says. "The king's secret must be kept." The wizard stands up. "Now, it is time to shine your dragons. Lets us go."

## Page 60

As they are leaving the Training Room, Rori runs over to Ana. She starts whispering to her. Drake keeps an eye on Rori as they walk toward the dragon caves. She has a sneaky look on her face, he thinks. What are she and Ana up to?
